How It Works

What is a Structurally Insulated Panel?

A SIP panel consists of two engineered timber skins (OSB) bonded under pressure to a rigid foam insulation core (EPS or polyurethane). The result is a single high-performance building element that replaces your wall framing, insulation, and vapour barrier — all in one.

Because panels are manufactured to precision in our factory, there is virtually no cutting or waste on site. Your build sequence is planned before a single panel arrives.

Common Questions

SIP Panel FAQ

How do SIP panels connect to each other?+
Panels connect using a spline system — either a timber spline, LVL spline, or foam-insulated spline depending on structural requirements. All joins are sealed for airtightness. Your panel layout design from Supersub includes all connection details.
Do SIP panels comply with NZ building code?+
Yes. Supersub SIP panels are engineered to comply with NZ Building Code requirements including NZS 3604 structural requirements and NZBC Clause H1 energy efficiency. Our technical team can provide engineering documentation for your consent.
How are electrical and plumbing run through SIP panels?+
Electrical chases can be factory-routed into panels before delivery. For plumbing, we typically design wet walls as conventional framed sections within the SIP structure. Our design team will advise on the best approach for your specific layout.
What is the minimum order size?+
We supply panels for projects of all sizes — from single rooms to full housing developments. Contact us with your project details and we'll provide a tailored quote.
How long does delivery take after ordering?+
Lead time is typically 4–6 weeks from confirmed order and finalised panel design. We can discuss expedited timelines for urgent projects — contact our team.
